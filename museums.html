Museums: Fundacio Joan Miro

Joan Miro is perhaps one of the most famous Spanish artists of all time, and one wouldn't want to miss this beautiful exhibition of his work. This museum, which was initiated by Joan Miro himself, opeed June 10th 1975 and features mostly his work. The museum owns over 10,000 paintings, scupltures, stage designs and carpets from Joan Miro. This museum is a can't miss if one loves the work of Joan Miro or would just like to simply learn more.

Museu Picasso de Barcelona

Oh, Picasso. Perhaps one of the most famous artists ever, Picasso had a large impact in the 20th century art scene. This exhibition holds more than 4,000 Picasso pieces of artwork, most of which are from his early years. This museum gives insight on Picasso's artistic development and shows his transitions as an artist into the style he is most well known for today. The majority of the pieces of art found in this museum were donated by Picasso himself. To enter this museum, there is a fee for a general admission ticket.

Museum of the History of Barcelona, or MUHBA

The Museum of the History of Barcelona is located inside the Palau Padellas, which was built in the 15th century. This museum host many unique exhibitions for visitorsto see the ancient ruins of Barcelona that have lasted over thousands of years. In the museum, visitors can follow a guided path to immerse themselves in Barcelona's rich ancient culture and learn more about the city's past.

Museu Nacional d'Art de Catalunya

The Museu Nacional d'Art de Catalunya is located in the Palau Nacional of Montjuic. This exhibition is the national museum of Catalan. This is a great starting point to learn more about the rich culture and history Barcelona has to offer. The museum has Romanesque paintings and Catalan art from the 19th and 20th century.

Museu d'Art Contemporani de Barcelona

This contemporary art museum located in the heart of Barcelona opened to the public on November 28, 1995. The current director since 2015 is Ferran Barenblit. The permanent collection of MACBA contains around 5,000 works. THere are three periods of modern art represented: the 1940s-60s, the 60s-70s, and current artwork.
