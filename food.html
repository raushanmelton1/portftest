Food: Paella

Arguably one of the most popular dishes found in Barcelona, Spain is paella. Paella actually originated in Valencia, but has since become a staple in the Spanish diet. Paella is typically cooked in a large, flat dish over a long period of time. Depending on the type of paella, different proteins are the main elements complemented with vegetables and spices. These proteins could include seafood, which might have shrimp, mussels, and clams. Other paellas might entain sausage, chicken or other meat. Spices such as saffron make this dish delectable and unique to Spanish cuisine.

Tapas

Tapas: a famous Spanish dish. A tapa is a small platter of food, not as large as a main course meal. Often in Spain, tapas are brought out alongside your drink and meant for a light bite. Going out for tapas is a popular activity in Spain. When a group orders tapas, these plates are typically shared. Tapas can be anything, and aren’t limited in variety. Tapas may include meats, cheeses, olives, meatballs, seasoned vegetables, and more. When abroad in Barcelona, one must try tapas for a true Spanish experience.

Cloudstreet Bakery

While there are many staples in the types of cuisine found in Spanish culture, there are also famous restaurants and bakeries one must try when they are in Barcelona. One famous bakery, Cloudstreet Bakery, is known for their sourdough bread. Using a restored wood oven from 1926 and imported spelt flour, this artisinal bakery is known for producing fresh, high-quality baked goods that one must check out when they go abroad.

Boutafumiero Barcelona

If one wants to indulge in fine dining, look no further than Botafumiero Barcelona. This restaurant is expensive, but is boasts that their fish is only the highest quality of seafood. They catch their fish fresh every day and are known for an unmatched taste. If you can’t afford this high-end establishment, dont fret: Barcelona is right along the ocean, so fresh fish is accessible even without the high tag dollar sign.
